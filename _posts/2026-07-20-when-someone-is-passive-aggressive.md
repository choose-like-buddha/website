The words say one thing; the tone says another. The silent treatment, the backhanded compliment, the "fine" that means anything but. You can feel the hostility, but it's wrapped in deniability, so when you name it, you're told you're imagining things. You're left off-balance, sensing the anger but unable to address it directly, exhausted by the effort of decoding what someone won't simply say.

## The Mindful Approach

Passive-aggression is anger that's afraid to be direct — hostility expressed sideways because the person hasn't learned, or doesn't feel safe enough, to express it openly. It's frustrating precisely because it denies you anything clear to respond to. The way through is not to play the same indirect game, but to meet the indirectness with calm, honest clarity.

- **Don't decode endlessly or take the bait.** Trying to interpret every sigh and silence keeps you entangled and anxious. You're not responsible for translating someone's unspoken resentment. You can notice it without organizing your whole inner life around solving the puzzle they refuse to state plainly.
- **Name the behavior gently and openly.** Without accusation, you can bring the indirect into the light: "You seem upset — I'd really rather you tell me directly so we can sort it out." This invites honesty and quietly declines to participate in the sideways game. Some people soften when the hidden anger is met with calm.
- **Stay steady when they deny it.** Often the passive-aggressive person will insist nothing's wrong. You don't have to argue them into admitting it. Simply hold your ground: "Okay — if something comes up, I'm here to talk about it directly." Then let it go, without absorbing the unspoken charge.

## A Practice for Today

The next time you sense passive-aggression, resist both the urge to retaliate in kind and the urge to anxiously decode it. Instead, calmly name what you notice and invite directness: "I get the sense something's bothering you — I'd genuinely like to hear it straight." Then stay peaceful regardless of their response. You cannot force someone to be direct, but you can refuse to be drawn into the riddle, and keep your own communication clean.
