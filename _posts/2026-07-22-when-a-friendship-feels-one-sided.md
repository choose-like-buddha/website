You're always the one who texts first, who plans, who remembers, who shows up. If you stopped reaching, you suspect the silence would simply stretch on forever. You care deeply, but you've started to feel less like a friend and more like a fan — investing in someone who accepts your effort warmly enough but never quite returns it. The imbalance has begun to ache.

## The Mindful Approach

Friendships breathe in and out; over time, the giving and receiving should roughly balance, even if never perfectly. A persistently one-sided friendship leaves the giver depleted and quietly wounded. The work is to look honestly at the imbalance — without bitterness — and decide what's true, rather than exhausting yourself trying to earn a reciprocity that isn't coming.

- **Look honestly at the pattern, not the exceptions.** It's easy to cling to the rare moments they showed up and ignore the steady tilt. Step back and ask what the relationship actually looks like over months. Patterns tell the truth that hopeful exceptions obscure.
- **Test the balance by gently stepping back.** Sometimes you can't see the shape of a friendship until you stop carrying it alone. Ease off the constant reaching and notice what happens. If they step toward you, there's life here. If only silence follows, that too is an answer worth hearing.
- **Release without resentment.** Some friendships are seasonal, or simply matter more to one person than the other — and that's nobody's crime. You can grieve the imbalance and adjust your investment accordingly, without anger. Not every connection is meant to be central, and that's allowed.

## A Practice for Today

Bring to mind the friendship that feels one-sided. Without bitterness, ask: have I been holding this up alone? Then experiment — let the next move be theirs, and see whether the space gets filled. Meanwhile, redirect some of that loyal energy toward the people who reach back. You don't have to cut anyone off in anger. You only have to stop pouring into a cup that was never going to be passed back to you.
