The maneuvering, the alliances, the unspoken games — it all leaves you uneasy. Some people seem to advance by playing them well, while you'd rather just do good work and be judged on it. But ignoring the politics entirely feels naive, and joining them feels like a betrayal of who you are. You're caught between staying clean and staying afloat, unsure how to move through a system that rewards the very things you dislike.

## The Mindful Approach

Office politics are, at root, the dynamics of power and relationship in any group of people — unavoidable wherever humans gather. The question isn't whether to engage but how. You can be aware of the currents and navigate them skillfully without abandoning your integrity. Wisdom here means neither naive avoidance nor cynical scheming, but clear-eyed honesty that protects both your interests and your character.

- **Be aware without becoming consumed.** Pretending politics don't exist leaves you vulnerable; obsessing over them makes you anxious and small. Understand the dynamics — who influences what, how decisions really get made — without letting the game occupy your whole mind. Awareness is protection, not a new identity.
- **Build genuine relationships, not transactional alliances.** The healthiest way to navigate politics is through authentic goodwill — treating people with respect, being reliable, building real trust. This is influence that doesn't cost your integrity, and it tends to outlast the maneuvering of those who only calculate.
- **Let integrity be your long game.** Manipulation and scheming may win in the short term, but a reputation for honesty and competence compounds over time. You don't have to play dirty to do well. Staying true to your values is not naivety; it's the strategy that lets you respect yourself no matter the outcome.

## A Practice for Today

Notice one workplace dynamic you've been either ignoring or stressing over. Bring clear awareness to it — who's involved, what's really at stake — without letting it consume you. Then choose one action grounded in integrity: a genuine relationship to invest in, an honest contribution to make visible, a scheme to decline. You can move wisely through the politics without becoming a politician. Your integrity is the one asset no maneuvering can take from you.
