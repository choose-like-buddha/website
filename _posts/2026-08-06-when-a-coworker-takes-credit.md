You did the work, and someone else stood in the light for it. They presented your idea as their own, accepted the praise, let the assumption stand. The injustice burns — not just the loss of recognition, but the brazenness of it. You replay the meeting, draft furious responses in your head, and feel the heat rise every time their name comes up attached to your effort.

## The Mindful Approach

Having your work claimed by another is a genuine injustice, and the anger is fair. But anger handled poorly tends to harm you more than them — making you look reactive while they stay composed. The skill is to address it with calm clarity, to protect your interests wisely, and to anchor your sense of worth in the work itself rather than in who happened to get the applause.

- **Respond from clarity, not heat.** The instinct is to confront furiously or to seethe in silence — both serve you poorly. A calm, factual approach is far more effective: documenting your contributions, ensuring the right people know your role, addressing it directly without accusation. Composure is power here; visible resentment is not.
- **Separate your worth from the recognition.** It stings to go unacknowledged, but your competence is real whether or not it was credited. Don't let someone else's theft make you doubt the value of what you did. The work happened; you know it happened; your capability is intact regardless of the applause.
- **Protect yourself going forward without becoming cynical.** Learn from it — keep records, communicate your contributions clearly, build visibility for your work. You can be wiser about protecting your efforts without letting one person's behavior turn you bitter or guarded toward everyone.

## A Practice for Today

If a coworker has taken credit, resist both the silent seething and the furious confrontation. Instead, plan one calm, clear action — a factual note to the relevant person, a direct but composed word with the coworker, a record of your contribution for next time. Remind yourself that your competence stands on its own, applause or not. Handle the injustice with steadiness, and you protect both your interests and your peace.
